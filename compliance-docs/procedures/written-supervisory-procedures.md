## 1. Overview

These Written Supervisory Procedures establish the framework for supervising all advisory activities at Meridian Wealth Advisors, including AI-powered advisory services, human oversight functions, and third-party integrations.

## 2. Supervisory Structure

### Chief Compliance Officer (CCO)
- **Reports to**: Firm principal / Board
- **Responsibilities**: Overall compliance program, regulatory filings, policy maintenance, examination preparation
- **Authority**: Can halt any client communication, trade, or marketing publication pending review

### AI Supervision Hierarchy
```
CCO (Human)
  └─ Compliance Agent Team (AI)
       ├─ Reviews: Financial Planning Agent output
       ├─ Reviews: Investment Management Agent trades
       ├─ Reviews: Client Support Agent communications
       └─ Reviews: Marketing Agent content
```

## 3. Client Account Supervision

### New Account Opening
1. Client completes onboarding questionnaire
2. AI validates data completeness and flags inconsistencies
3. Identity verification (KYC) completed via custodian
4. Risk tolerance assessment reviewed by Compliance Agent
5. Suitability determination documented
6. Advisory agreement executed
7. CCO samples 25% of new accounts monthly for quality review

### Ongoing Account Monitoring
- **Daily**: Automated portfolio drift monitoring, trade surveillance
- **Weekly**: CCO review of flagged items from AI compliance agent
- **Monthly**: CCO review of client communications sample (10%)
- **Quarterly**: Full account review cycle (25% of accounts per quarter)
- **Annually**: Every client account receives comprehensive review

### Suitability Reviews
Triggered by:
- New account opening
- Material change in client circumstances
- Client request for strategy change
- Significant market event affecting portfolio
- Annual review cycle

Documentation required:
- Client's current financial situation
- Investment objectives and time horizon
- Risk tolerance and capacity assessment
- Rationale for recommended strategy
- Alternatives considered

## 4. Trading Supervision

### Pre-Trade Compliance
- AI compliance check: Verify trade is consistent with client IPS
- Restricted list screening (if applicable)
- Wash sale rule monitoring (30-day lookback)
- Concentration limits check (no single security >10% of portfolio)
- Cash availability verification

### Post-Trade Review
- Best execution analysis: Compare execution prices to available market
- Error trade detection and correction procedures
- Trade allocation fairness review (for block trades)
- End-of-day reconciliation with custodian records

### Prohibited Trading Activities
- Trading ahead of client orders (front-running)
- Excessive trading (churning) - monitored by turnover ratio thresholds
- Unauthorized trading (trades without discretionary authority or client approval)
- Cross-trading between client accounts

## 5. Communications Supervision

### Client Communications (All Channels)
- All communications archived in real-time
- AI compliance agent scans for:
  - Performance guarantees or promissory language
  - Missing required disclosures
  - Potential misrepresentation
  - Unauthorized investment advice
  - Client complaints or expressions of dissatisfaction
- Flagged communications reviewed by CCO within 24 hours
- Monthly random sample review: 10% of all communications

### Marketing & Advertising
- All marketing materials require Compliance Agent pre-approval
- CCO reviews and approves materials flagged for elevated review
- Review checks:
  - [ ] No false or misleading statements
  - [ ] Performance presentations comply with Marketing Rule
  - [ ] Required disclaimers included
  - [ ] AI capabilities described accurately
  - [ ] Testimonials/endorsements comply with new Marketing Rule
- Approved materials archived with date and reviewer notation

## 6. Complaint Handling

### Definition
A complaint is any written or verbal expression of dissatisfaction by a client regarding the firm's services, personnel, or AI advisory systems.

### Procedure
1. Log complaint immediately in complaint register
2. Acknowledge receipt to client within 24 hours
3. CCO notified within 4 hours of receipt
4. Investigation initiated within 1 business day
5. Resolution target: 30 calendar days
6. Written response to client documenting resolution
7. Complaint and resolution archived for 5+ years
8. Quarterly complaint trend analysis by CCO

### Escalation
- Any complaint alleging fraud, theft, or unauthorized activity: Immediate CCO and legal counsel notification
- Any complaint filed with a regulatory body: Immediate CCO and legal counsel notification
- Pattern of similar complaints: Root cause analysis and remediation plan

## 7. Regulatory Filings & Examinations

### Routine Filings
| Filing | Deadline | Responsible |
|---|---|---|
| Form ADV Annual Amendment | Within 90 days of fiscal year end | CCO |
| Form ADV Other-Than-Annual Amendment | Promptly upon material change | CCO |
| Form CRS updates | When information becomes materially inaccurate | CCO |
| Privacy Policy delivery | Annual, to all clients | Operations |
| State notice filings | Per state requirements | CCO |

### Examination Preparation
- Maintain "exam-ready" status at all times
- Quarterly mock examination of random compliance areas
- Document request list template maintained and updated
- Designated exam coordinator: CCO

## 8. Annual Compliance Review

Per Rule 206(4)-7, the CCO conducts an annual review of:
- Adequacy of compliance policies and procedures
- Effectiveness of AI compliance monitoring
- Material compliance matters that occurred during the year
- Changes in business activities requiring policy updates
- Regulatory developments affecting the firm

The annual review is documented in writing and presented to firm principal.
