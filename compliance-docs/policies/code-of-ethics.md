## 1. Statement of General Principles

Meridian Wealth Advisors operates under a fiduciary standard. This Code of Ethics establishes the standards of conduct expected of all persons associated with the firm, including AI advisory systems. The following principles guide all firm activities:

1. **Client interest first**: The interests of clients take precedence over the interests of the firm and its personnel.
2. **Integrity**: All business activities conducted with honesty and good faith.
3. **Compliance**: Adherence to all applicable federal and state securities laws.
4. **Confidentiality**: Client information is private and protected.
5. **Professionalism**: Conduct that reflects well on the firm and the advisory profession.
6. **Transparency**: Open and honest communication with clients about services, fees, conflicts, and the use of AI technology.

## 2. Scope

This Code applies to:
- All supervised persons of the firm
- Human oversight personnel (CCO, operations staff)
- AI advisory systems (through programmatic enforcement)
- Third-party contractors with access to client information

## 3. Fiduciary Duty

All advisory activities must satisfy the fiduciary duty of care and loyalty:

**Duty of Care**:
- Provide advice suitable to each client's financial situation and goals
- Conduct reasonable investigation before making recommendations
- Monitor client portfolios and update recommendations as circumstances change

**Duty of Loyalty**:
- Act in the client's best interest at all times
- Disclose all material conflicts of interest
- Do not place firm interests ahead of client interests
- Seek best execution for client transactions

## 4. Personal Securities Transactions

### Applicability
This section applies to human oversight personnel ("access persons") who have access to nonpublic information about client trades or holdings.

### Requirements
- Pre-clearance required for personal trades in individual securities
- Blackout period: No personal trading in a security 3 business days before or after client trading in the same security
- Quarterly transaction reports submitted to CCO within 30 days of quarter end
- Annual holdings report submitted to CCO within 45 days of calendar year end
- Prohibited: Front-running, insider trading, market manipulation

### AI Systems
- AI agents do not hold personal securities positions
- AI trading algorithms are audited quarterly for unauthorized activity
- All AI-initiated trades are logged and available for compliance review

## 5. Conflicts of Interest

### Identified Conflicts
- Fee-based compensation creates incentive to grow AUM [mitigated by flat-tier pricing]
- Custodian selection may be influenced by service quality to the firm [mitigated by multi-custodian approach]

### Conflict Management
- All identified conflicts disclosed in Form ADV Part 2A
- Annual conflict of interest review conducted by CCO
- New conflicts documented and disclosed within 30 days
- AI recommendation engine audited for systematic bias

## 6. Gifts and Entertainment

Human oversight personnel:
- May not accept gifts valued over $100 from any person or entity doing business with the firm
- Must report all gifts received to the CCO within 5 business days
- May not give gifts intended to influence any person's business decisions

## 7. Confidentiality

- All client information is confidential and may not be disclosed except as described in the Privacy Policy
- Firm proprietary information (AI models, trading strategies, business plans) is confidential
- Confidentiality obligations survive termination of employment

## 8. Outside Business Activities

Human oversight personnel must disclose and receive prior approval for:
- Outside employment or business activities
- Service on boards of directors
- Private investments that may create conflicts

## 9. Reporting Violations

- Any person aware of a Code violation must report it to the CCO immediately
- Reports may be made anonymously
- No retaliation against good-faith reporters
- CCO investigates all reports and documents findings
- Material violations reported to SEC as required

## 10. Sanctions

Violations may result in:
- Written warning
- Disgorgement of profits from prohibited transactions
- Suspension
- Termination
- Referral to regulatory authorities

## 11. Acknowledgment

All access persons must:
- Receive a copy of this Code upon hire/association
- Acknowledge receipt in writing
- Certify compliance annually
- Report any violations promptly
